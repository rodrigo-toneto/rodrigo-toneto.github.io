Welcome

I am a PhD student in Economics at Queen Mary University of London, supervised by Lucie Gadenne, and a researcher at the Institute for Applied Economic Research (IPEA) in Brazil.

My research lies at the intersection of public economics and labor markets, with a focus on social pensions, minimum wages, and the fiscal and distributional impacts of public policy.

About me

My current work uses large-scale administrative datasets (RAIS, INSS, Estban) to study the effects of social pensions on local labor markets, the distributional impacts of minimum wage policies, and the fiscal capacity of Brazilian municipalities. I am broadly interested in how social protection systems interact with labor market formalization, economic development, and fiscal sustainability.
